# Link Board

Build me a link-sharing community platform — think Hacker News, but our own.

The idea is simple: people sign up, post interesting links they've found around
the web, and the community votes the best stuff to the top. Good posts rise,
noise sinks. Underneath every link is a conversation, so people can discuss and
reply to each other. Over time, members build up a reputation based on how the
community receives what they share and say.

Here's the kind of experience I'm picturing:

- **People have accounts.** Someone can join the community, sign in, and come
  back later to the same identity. The things they do are tied to who they are,
  and only they can act as themselves.

- **People share links.** A member can submit a link with a headline, and
  everyone can browse what's been shared. I want the front page to feel alive —
  fresh submissions, but also a sense of what's genuinely popular versus just
  new.

- **The community votes.** Members can express approval or disapproval of a
  link, and that collective judgment is what decides ranking. One person, one
  say per item — and they can change their mind.

- **Every link has a discussion.** People can comment on a link and reply to
  each other, so conversations can branch into threads. Comments can be voted
  on too, and the good replies should stand out.

- **Members have reputation and a public presence.** Each member has a profile
  others can look at — who they are, when they joined, what they've contributed,
  and a reputation score that reflects how the community has received their
  contributions. People can tell others a bit about themselves.

- **The community can police itself.** Members should be able to flag things
  that don't belong. There should be a way to find past content by searching for
  it. And someone needs to be in charge — a moderator who can remove bad content
  and deal with members who abuse the platform.

Use your judgment on everything else. It's a backend service — an API that a web
or mobile front end could sit on top of later. Make it solid, sensible, and hard
to break.
